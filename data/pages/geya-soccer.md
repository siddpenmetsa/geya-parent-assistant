GEYA Soccer is an all-volunteer recreational soccer program.

GEYA Soccer divisions:
- Boys: Kindergarten, 1st Grade, 2nd/3rd Grade, 4th/5th Grade, and 6th-8th Grade.
- Girls: Kindergarten, 1st Grade, 2nd/3rd Grade, 4th/5th Grade, and 6th-8th Grade.
- Coed: 9th-12th Grade.
- HIGH 5 Soccer: players with special needs.

GEYA Soccer offers a spring season, a fall season, and winter indoor futsal. Teams are coached by volunteer coaches, most of whom are parents of players on the team.

GEYA Soccer uses TeamSnap for registration and team management. Every parent should download TeamSnap, create an individual account, and then use the current season registration link.

GEYA provides separate TeamSnap links for Kindergarten through 12th Grade divisions, volunteer coaches, and HIGH 5 Soccer.

After registration ends, GEYA Soccer needs about 10 days to build rosters and assign coaches. Team rosters and schedules are then published, and families are notified of team assignments through TeamSnap.

Coaches send first-practice information through TeamSnap. All divisions practice twice before the first game except Coed, which practices once.

Schedules are posted to TeamSnap once rosters are built, roughly 10 days before first practices.

Each soccer team plays or practices twice each week. One event is on a weeknight from Monday through Thursday, and one event is on Saturday morning or early afternoon. The weeknight event changes from week to week.

GEYA Soccer equipment and player expectations:
- Each player should bring a properly inflated soccer ball with their name on it.
- Ball size 3 is for Kindergarten and 1st Grade.
- Ball size 4 is for 2nd/3rd Grade and 4th/5th Grade.
- Ball size 5 is for 6th-8th Grade and Coed.
- Each player must wear shin guards under socks for all practices and games.
- Cleats are recommended.
- Individual water bottles are recommended, and sharing water bottles should be limited.
- Earrings are not permitted. Coaches will ask players to remove them before participating. If they resist, they cannot play.

Weather decisions are typically made 90 minutes before an event starts. If families do not receive league communication by email or text, the event is still on.

GEYA Soccer game conduct:
- 4th-12th Grade divisions have paid referees who observe normal soccer rules for throw-ins and goal kicks.
- For Kindergarten and 1st Grade, coaches enforce field boundaries reasonably during the first month, then more strictly as the season continues.
- For 2nd and 3rd Grade, coaches enforce boundaries strictly from the start.
- Kindergarten and 1st Grade use kick-ins. 2nd and 3rd Grade use throw-ins.
- For K-5 goal kicks, the defensive team must retreat to the buildout line and allow one pass before pressuring the ball.
- Players should receive at least one substitution per quarter or half.
- All players should play at least 50 percent of the game and have the opportunity to play multiple positions.
- Coaches can adapt the number of players on the field sensibly according to attendance.
