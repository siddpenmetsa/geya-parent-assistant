GEYA Little League Baseball divisions:
- Tee Ball.
- Minor - Coach/Machine Pitch.
- Minor - Player Pitch.
- Major.
- Intermediate.
- Junior.
- Senior.

Tee Ball is the entry-level division for players ages 4-7. Tee Ball is grounded in fun, fitness, and fundamentals. Tee Ball players use bats and balls designated specifically for the division. Tee Ball roster size averages 7-10 players. The season usually includes 1-2 practices per week and one weekend game.

Each Little Leaguer is required to play one season of Tee Ball before being eligible for evaluation by local league officials for consideration to advance to a higher Little League baseball or softball division.

Minor Coach/Machine Pitch is intended for League Age 5-8 players for a full season of Coach/Machine Pitch baseball. It is primarily for League Age 7 players with some advanced 5- and 6-year-olds and young or new 8-year-olds. League Age 5- and 6-year-olds who have completed one year of Tee Ball can participate in Minor Coach/Machine Pitch.

Minor Player Pitch is for ages 8-10. Minor League Baseball programs may be operated for younger players with less experience. The minor league may include players ages 5-11, and divisions can include machine pitch, coach pitch, or player pitch. Rosters may have 12-20 players, with most rosters in the 12-15 range. The diamond is 60 feet, and the pitching distance is 46 feet.

Major, also called Little League Baseball Major Division, is for boys and girls ages 9-12. A local league may choose to limit Major Division to 10-, 11-, and 12-year-olds or to 11-12-year-olds. The diamond is 60 feet, and the pitching distance is 46 feet.

Intermediate 50/70 is for players league-age 11-13. It uses a 50-foot pitching distance and 70-foot base paths. It is a transition between the standard Little League field size and the Junior/Senior field size.

Junior is for ages 12-14 and uses a 60/90 full-size field.

Senior is for ages 13-16 and uses a 60/90 full-size field.

Parents are strongly encouraged to volunteer in Little League. After completing a Little League volunteer application and passing a required national background check, parents may help with practices and may be eligible as coaches, managers, umpires, local league board members, and other volunteer positions.
